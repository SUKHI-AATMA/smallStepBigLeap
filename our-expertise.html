Cardiovascular

Heart related ailments have remained the leading cause of death around the world over the last two decades. As Indians, we are more prone to them for a variety of reasons: rising stress, a fibre-deficient high carbohydrate and trans- fat diet, excess salt consumption, lack of regular exercise, propensity to binge on sweets, fried food, genetics…

A hectic and unhealthy lifestyle with increasing stress, irregular eating habits, rising intake of fast foods, and negligence in a proper diet regimen plus unhealthy habits – consumption of alcohol and tobacco further catalyse the risks.

Digestion and absorption of all food and nutrients is managed by our gut. Home to millions of good bacteria and immune cells, a healthy gut wards of infections and communicates seamlessly with the brain, enabling us to maintain our overall wellbeing.

Any imbalance in key macronutrients, micronutrients and water can lead to digestive ailments. Hectic lifestyle with irregular food habits, increasing stress, rising intake of fast foods and negligence in a proper diet regimen often leads to digestive disorders and diseases. Unhealthy habits – consumption of alcohol, tobacco and lack of right physical exercise further exacerbate it.

Mental
  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Schizophrenia
  • Attention deficit
  • Autism

The brain and gut are closely interconnected via various communication pathways running through nerves, blood, autonomic nervous system and hypothalamic-pituitary adrenal axis. Medical researches and surveys show evidences of strong co-relations between anxiety, depression and functional gastrointestinal disorders and vice versa. An alteration in the gut microbiota in the neuropsychiatric diseases can be a reason for behavioural abnormalities. A healthy microbiome in the gut helps in building a good immune system with positive influence on our behaviour and emotions.

Family
  • Obesity

  • Malnutrition & related disorders

The key to happiness is good health, which directly relates to what we eat.

Increasing preference for ordering food, eating only what one likes, meals at irregular times, increased binging of fast foods plus the innate fixation for all things digital with limited physical activity is creating havoc.

Families though staying together under one roof, are getting disconnected due to over dependence on digital both for work as well as entertainment. The good old tradition of families sitting around the table, enjoying home cooked meals, sharing & spending time together is shrinking.

Several emerging lifestyle disorders – lack of sleep, obesity, feeling of anxiety, depression, inertia, low immunity and malnutrition origin from an innate lack of proper nutrition and physical activity.

Women’s
  • Adolescence
  • Pregnancy – Pre, During & Post
  • Menopause

Anatomically, a woman undergoes several more hormonal changes, during her life cycle than a man. These directly impact her overall physical and mental growth as well as her emotional and psychological wellbeing. A well balanced, nutritious diet, at each stage of her life, is hence vital for a woman.

Unfortunately, both culturally and historically, health and nutrition of women has not been a priority in the Indian milieu resulting in low levels of awareness and detestable practices regarding their food habits.

In todays’ urban setting, always multi-tasking while playing several roles both in her professional and personal life, often leaves little time and energy for a woman to invest in her own health. The ad-hoc adoption of quick fix solutions via fad diets, skipping meals and taking supplements towards desired weight focus further muddles an already imperfect condition.
